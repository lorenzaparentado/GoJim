Core Exercises

Plank

Start in the correct position: Begin in a high plank position, with your hands directly underneath your shoulders and your feet hip-width apart. Engage your core: Tighten your core muscles by drawing your belly button in towards your spine. Keep your back straight: Maintain a straight line from your head to your heels. Avoid letting your hips sag or lifting your butt up too high. Don't lock your elbows: Keep a slight bend in your elbows to avoid putting too much pressure on your joints. Focus on your breathing: Take deep breaths in and out through your nose, focusing on keeping your breath steady and controlled.

Reverse Crunch

Lie flat on your back: Start by lying flat on your back on a mat or padded surface. Bend your knees: Bend your knees and lift your feet off the ground, so that your thighs are perpendicular to the ground. Position your hands: Place your hands by your sides, with your palms facing down. Engage your core: Tighten your abdominal muscles by drawing your belly button towards your spine. Lift your hips: Lift your hips off the ground towards your chest, curling your pelvis towards your ribcage. Lower your hips: Slowly lower your hips back down to the starting position, keeping your core engaged the entire time. Don't use momentum: Avoid using momentum to lift your hips. Focus on using your abdominal muscles to perform the movement. Keep your movements slow and controlled: Perform the exercise in a slow and controlled manner, avoiding any jerky or sudden movements. Don't strain your neck: Avoid straining your neck by keeping it in a neutral position and focusing on lifting your hips using your core muscles. Breathe: Inhale as you lower your hips back down to the starting position, and exhale as you lift your hips up towards your chest.

Bicycle Crunches

Lie flat on your back: Start by lying flat on your back on a mat or padded surface. Position your hands: Place your hands behind your head, lightly supporting your head with your fingers. Lift your legs: Lift your legs off the ground, keeping your knees bent at a 90-degree angle. Engage your core: Tighten your abdominal muscles by drawing your belly button towards your spine. Twist your torso: Twist your torso to bring your right elbow towards your left knee, straightening your right leg out at the same time. Repeat on the other side: Return to the starting position and then twist your torso to bring your left elbow towards your right knee, straightening your left leg out at the same time. Keep your movements slow and controlled: Perform the exercise in a slow and controlled manner, avoiding any jerky or sudden movements. Don't pull on your neck: Avoid pulling on your neck with your hands. Instead, use your core muscles to lift your upper body off the ground. Breathe: Inhale as you twist your torso, and exhale as you return to the starting position.

Leg Raises

Lie flat on your back: Start by lying flat on your back on a mat or padded surface. Position your hands: Place your hands by your sides, with your palms facing down. Engage your core: Tighten your abdominal muscles by drawing your belly button towards your spine. Lift your legs: Lift both legs off the ground, keeping them straight. Keep your legs together: Keep your legs together and avoid bending your knees. Lower your legs: Slowly lower your legs back down to the starting position, keeping your core engaged the entire time. Don't arch your back: Avoid arching your back or lifting your hips off the ground. Focus on using your core muscles to perform the movement. Keep your movements slow and controlled: Perform the exercise in a slow and controlled manner, avoiding any jerky or sudden movements. Don't strain your neck: Avoid straining your neck by keeping it in a neutral position and focusing on lifting your legs using your core muscles. Breathe: Inhale as you lower your legs back down to the starting position, and exhale as you lift your legs up towards the ceiling.
