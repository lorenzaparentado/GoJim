Quad Exercises

Front Squats

Begin by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart and the barbell resting on the front of your shoulders, with your palms facing up and your elbows pointed forward. Keeping your core engaged and your chest up, slowly lower your body by bending your knees and hips, pushing your butt back and down. Keep your back straight and your knees in line with your toes as you lower yourself down until your thighs are parallel to the ground. Pause for a moment at the bottom of the squat and then push through your heels to stand back up to the starting position. Keep your movements slow and controlled, and avoid arching your back or leaning forward excessively. Breathe in as you lower your body, and exhale as you push back up to the starting position. Use a weight that allows you to perform the exercise with good form and without straining. As you progress, gradually increase the weight and/or the number of repetitions. Finally, make sure to stretch your legs and hips after your workout to prevent soreness and promote recovery.

Leg Extensions

Adjust the seat height so that the knee pad is aligned with the top of your ankle. Sit on the seat with your back flat against the pad and your feet flat on the ground. Adjust the weight according to your fitness level and goals. Place your lower legs under the padded bar, and grip the handles with your hands. Slowly lift the padded bar by extending your legs, making sure to keep your core engaged and your back straight. Hold the contraction for a moment at the top of the movement, and then slowly lower the bar back down to the starting position. Keep your movements slow and controlled, and avoid jerking or swinging the padded bar.

Leg Press

Adjust the seat position so that your hips and knees are at a 90-degree angle when your feet are on the platform. Place your feet shoulder-width apart on the platform, with your toes pointing slightly outward. Hold onto the handles on the side of the machine for stability. Slowly bend your knees and lower the platform until your thighs are parallel to the ground, keeping your back flat against the seat. Pause for a moment at the bottom of the movement, and then push through your heels to straighten your legs and lift the platform back up to the starting position. Keep your movements slow and controlled, and avoid locking your knees at the top of the movement.
